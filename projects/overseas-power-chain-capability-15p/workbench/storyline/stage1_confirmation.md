## Storyline Options

### Option A: Closed-Loop Scenario Construction

- Situation: overseas power industry chain enterprises need reusable capability proof across bidding, financing, supply-chain, country-entry, and public-service contexts.
- Complication: proof materials are scattered, rules are inconsistent, and external stakeholders increasingly need structured and credible evaluation outputs.
- Resolution: build a unified "evidence collection - evaluation generation - scenario application - continuous operation" scenario led by CEC and operated through data infrastructure.
- Evidence IDs: E03, E04, E05, E07, E08, E09, E10, E15, E16.
- Strength: strongest fit to the provided 15-page script; naturally supports architecture, scenarios, operations, and risk pages.
- Caveat: external policy and market evidence is not embedded.
- Recommendation: use this as the primary storyline.

### Option B: Governance-Led Public Service Platform

- Situation: the industry needs common rules and public service outputs for enterprise overseas development.
- Complication: individual enterprises and external institutions cannot easily align evidence, indicators, result forms, and authorization boundaries.
- Resolution: position CEC as the rule organizer and public-service coordinator, with digital company operation and expert review.
- Evidence IDs: E05, E06, E07, E14, E15, E16.
- Strength: clear governance logic and leadership-facing value.
- Caveat: may underplay the concrete application scenarios in pages 10-13.
- Recommendation: use as a secondary emphasis, especially in P5 and P14.

### Option C: Scenario Productization and Service Layering

- Situation: evaluation results need to become reusable products and services.
- Complication: without scenario packaging, evaluation outputs remain static reports and cannot enter real workflows.
- Resolution: launch layered services: foundation profile, special evaluation, monitoring/subscription, international communication, and ecosystem collaboration.
- Evidence IDs: E10, E11, E12, E13, E14, E15.
- Strength: strong operational and product logic.
- Caveat: if used as the main storyline, it may weaken the "why now" and rule-building rationale.
- Recommendation: keep as the third-chapter and fourth-chapter execution spine, not the whole-deck spine.

## Recommended SCR

- Situation: overseas development by power industry chain enterprises is deepening, and external cooperation increasingly requires credible, reusable capability proof.
- Complication: current materials, evidence, rules, and result expressions are scattered across organizations and scenarios, making capability proof hard to reuse and hard to trust.
- Resolution: build a CEC-led overseas development capability evaluation scenario with unified entrance, evidence directory, evaluation model, scenario outputs, and continuous operation mechanism.
